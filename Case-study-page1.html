UI Redesign of the Canadian Space Agency Site with Navigation Prototype

Project Overview

The Canadian Space agency or CSA, is responsible for providing communications to the public about advancements in space sciences. As well, the CSA sponsors research in space related topics to universities and other organizations. Finally, the promotes space as a relevant industry with major economic benefit to the Canadian public, through educational services and active recruitment.

Learn more

Roles and Responsibilities

During the research and ideation phases, I partnered with Licia Girao-UX designer. We did a heuristic analysis of the current site, conducted and collected data from usability tests, performed research analysis. In the ideation phase we developed the wireframes for the redesigned navigation.
Other collaborative efforts included making the moodboard and rebranding such as the new logo. It was during the prototyping and retesting stages where we set off on our own paths to build out our own unique visions for how the new CSA site, desktop and mobile versions would take shape.

Purpose for the Redesign

We believe that to improve public engagement for the Canadian Space Agency(CSA), modernizing the CSA site would help with this. Enabling fast and clear navigation would help users from a non-scientific background be drawn in finding out about the agency’s projects and its benefits to Canadians. Further, the redesign helps to clarify the CSA’s reason for being to a curious or skeptical public who might balk at significant amounts of tax money to pay for this program.
